# The Crowd Signal — product spec

## What it is
A foresight property. Two layers:
1. **Terminal** (existing dashboard) — live, read-only data: what the crowd is
   pricing now across categories. This is the credibility / data layer.
2. **Signal Brief** (new editorial layer) — short, regular, forward-looking
   reads that synthesise prediction-market moves + news into *"what the crowd
   expects next and why it matters."*

The Brief is the differentiator. Almost nobody turns prediction-market data
into clean editorial foresight — the space is raw dashboards or gambling tips.
This is neither: it is honest synthesis.

## Honest positioning
Not predictions, not betting advice, not financial advice. We report what the
collective is pricing and the visible context. The reader draws conclusions.
Every brief carries the standing disclaimer.

## Audience
Curious, intelligent readers who follow markets / tech / geopolitics and want
*signal, not noise* — the "what is the world expecting next" reader. Not
gamblers.

## Format (quality bar)
- 600–900 words, scannable.
- Structure: 1-line hook → 3–5 "Signals" (what moved · the number · why it
  matters · honest caveat) → "What we're watching" close → disclaimer.
- No hype, no fake urgency, no advice verbs ("bet", "buy"). Editorial, calm,
  precise. Numbers always sourced from the live snapshot.

## Cadence
2× per week to start (sustainable solo + AI). Consistency > volume.

## Distribution (no DMs, compounds with time)
- Published as static pages on the existing GitHub Pages site (SEO-indexable,
  shareable, zero new platform, zero cost, auto-deployed via existing Action).
- Growth = consistent quality output ranking + getting shared organically.
  This is the user's time converted into distribution.

## Monetisation (later, only after organic traffic)
Free archive builds the audience. Then: premium tier (deeper/earlier briefs +
data access), a single sponsor slot, or licensed data. NOT before traffic.

## Honest risks
Slow (3–6+ months or never). AI-content SEO headwinds → only depth/uniqueness
survives, never volume spam. Requires sustained cadence even though Claude
generates most of it. No guarantee — best fit to constraints, not a promise.

## Division of labour
- Claude: data pipeline, write every brief from real data, layout, deploy prep.
- User: keep the cadence (approve/publish), own the accounts. No selling.

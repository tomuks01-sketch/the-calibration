Crowd Signal #007 · 13 June 2026 · 2 min read

A coin flip, and a calibrated band

We have now auto-scored 49 of our 24-hour crypto forecasts against a random-walk baseline. The honest verdict, on the public record: our up/down calls are a coin flip — exactly as we warned — while our volatility band is well calibrated. We are publishing both halves, the unflattering one first.

1 · What went on the record

Since early June the system has opened about seven crypto forecasts a day on the most liquid coins. Each is locked at the moment it is made — its price and timestamp written down — and graded automatically 24 hours later against a random-walk baseline (a forecaster that always says "50/50, no expected move"). Two numbers are scored: a probability the coin closes higher (Brier score vs 0.5) and an 80% volatility band (did the outcome land inside it). Forty-nine windows have now closed.

2 · Direction: a coin flip

Our probability-of-up beat a 50/50 baseline by 0.0019 in Brier terms — which is to say, not at all. Directional accuracy was 53.1% across the 49 windows, indistinguishable from chance at this sample size (the window itself drifted down — only ~45% of days closed up). We said from the first day that 24-hour direction in liquid crypto is essentially unforecastable, and the scoreboard now says it plainly. No edge was claimed, and none was found. Most products would bury this number. It is our headline.

3 · The band that holds

The genuinely forecastable thing is not which way but how far. Our 80% volatility band — the range we expect the price to stay within over 24 hours — covered 81.6% of the 49 outcomes, against a target of 80%. That is the part of this that works: an honestly sized measure of uncertainty, calibrated to within two points of its claim. It makes no directional promise and earns no profit; it simply tells you how wide the next day plausibly is, and so far it has been right about that.

4 · What this does — and doesn't — mean

Forty-nine windows is an early read, not a verdict. A small directional edge, if one existed, would need hundreds of (heavily correlated) windows before it could even be detected — so "coin flip" today is the expected result, not a failure. The band's calibration is encouraging but it is a few weeks of data, not a law. We are publishing now — coin-flip direction and all — because the whole point is to be scored in public before we are sure, not after. Every number here is on the Crypto page, updating as the record grows.

5 · The prediction-market ledger

Separately, the Polymarket Scoreboard still shows 0 resolved of 18 pending. Those markets settle on their own timeline, and the record fills when they do; the crypto forecasts simply resolve every 24 hours, which is why they have a scorecard first. No score to show there yet is the honest state, and it is shown.

Analytics only. These are transparent, documented forecasts scored in public against a random-walk baseline — not predictions to act on, not betting, financial or investment advice, and no profit claim. A positive volatility-band coverage is not an edge; a coin-flip direction is not a recommendation. Numbers are from the live record and move continuously. Think for yourself.

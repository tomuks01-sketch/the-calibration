How the model works

  • Elo — each nation's strength from ~49k international results (bigger games move ratings more; home advantage included).
  • Poisson goals — the Elo gap maps to expected goals for each side, giving the full scoreline distribution: win/draw/win, the likely score, over-2.5 and both-teams-score.
  • Scored — locked before kickoff, graded with RPS vs the result and vs the market's odds. We only claim to beat the market when the record proves it.

These are documented, reproducible probabilities and scenarios — never "team X will win", never betting advice. The model's constants are honest priors until a resolved track record earns calibration. International results history is public (openfootball / community datasets); fixtures and odds via a public endpoint.

Football forecasts are analytics, scored in public against the result and the market. They are not predictions to act on, not betting or investment advice, and make no profit claim. Think for yourself.
